Medications

Critical safety note
Do not take Percocet and Klonopin at the same time. Follow Dr. Movahed’s pain management schedule exactly. Log every dose and the time it was given.

Daily schedule

Resets automatically each day

Pain management

Percocet(oxycodone-acetaminophen 5-325mg tablet)

  • ½ to 1 tablet by mouth every 4 to 6 hours as needed for pain. Maximum 4 tablets per day.
  • Take on schedule, especially the first few days. Don’t wait for pain to spike before taking a dose.
  • Log every dose on the whiteboard: medication name, dose, and exact time given.
  • Never combine with Klonopin. The two must not be taken at the same time under any circumstances.
  • 0 refills. This is a controlled substance.

Anti-nausea

Zofran ODT(ondansetron 4mg dissolving tablet)

  • Dissolve 1 tablet on the tongue every 6 hours as needed for nausea.
  • No water needed. The tablet dissolves on its own.
  • 20-day supply.

Muscle relaxer

Klonopin(clonazepam 1mg tablet)

  • 1 tablet at night for sleep and jaw clenching.
  • 30-day supply. Minimum 1 month post-op. Some patients need longer.
  • Must be properly tapered off. Never stop abruptly. This can cause serious withdrawal effects.
  • Do not take at the same time as Percocet.

Antibiotic

Augmentin(amoxicillin-clavulanate 600-42.9mg/5ml suspension)

  • 5ml by mouth every 12 hours. 10-day supply.
  • This is a liquid suspension. Shake well before each dose.
  • Fill immediately after hospital discharge. Do not wait.
  • Complete the entire 10-day course. Do not stop early even if I’m feeling better.

Oral rinse

Peridex(chlorhexidine gluconate 0.12% mouthwash)

  • 15ml (1 capful) swished for 30 seconds, morning and evening after brushing. Then spit.
  • After every meal: rinse with warm salt water first, then follow with Peridex.
  • 30-day supply. Critical for preventing infection around the sutures.

Sinus care

Congestion is normal and can last for months after MMA surgery.

Navage sinus rinse

  • Start 1 week post-op.
  • Use while seated at the sink. Do not use in the shower — risk of slipping while on pain medications.

Flonase and Astepro

  • Flonase: use at bedtime.
  • Astepro (azelastine nasal spray): use at bedtime for allergies.

Dairy

  • Dr. Movahed’s guide recommends avoiding dairy for the first month to reduce mucus and congestion.
The link between dairy and mucus production is debated. Multiple peer-reviewed studies found no increase in nasal secretions after milk consumption, and a 2005 review concluded milk does not lead to mucus production. The perceived effect may be due to milk’s texture coating the throat rather than actual mucus increase. However, a 2019 blinded trial did find that people with existing mucus complaints reported reduced symptoms on a dairy-free diet. Given that I’ll already have significant nasal congestion from the surgery, following Dr. Movahed’s recommendation to avoid dairy is the safer choice.

Supplements

Thorne Vitamin C 500mg

  • Take twice daily.
  • Always take alongside collagen — Vitamin C is required for collagen synthesis and significantly enhances absorption.

Ritual Men’s 18+ multivitamin

  • 2 capsules, once daily.

Probiotics

  • Take as directed. Especially important while on Augmentin to maintain gut health.
