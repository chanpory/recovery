Surgery day checklist

Thursday, April 9. Check-in at Alta Bates Berkeley at 5:30 AM.

The night before

  • Nothing to eat or drink after midnight, including water
  • Get a good night of sleep
  • Set alarm for ~4:30 AM
  • Lay out comfortable zip-up or button-up clothes (nothing that goes over the head)
  • Confirm ride to the hospital

Morning of

  • Shower with antibacterial soap
  • No makeup, moisturizers, lotions, perfumes, or deodorant
  • Wear glasses, not contacts
  • Leave all jewelry and valuables at home
  • Brush teeth and use mouthwash, but do not swallow any water
  • Clean shaven
  • If instructed to take morning medications, use only a tiny sip of water

What to bring

  • Photo ID and insurance card
  • Phone and charger with a long cord
  • Glasses case
  • Slip-on shoes or slippers for walking the hospital floor
  • Lip balm (lips get very dry)
  • The black bag from Dr. Movahed (ice packs, syringes, squeeze bottles, whiteboard)
  • Kate’s Farms shakes to use instead of the hospital’s Ensure
  • ArticIce machine

What not to bring

  • Contact lenses
  • Jewelry, piercings, hairpins
  • Anything valuable

For Harold

  • You don't need to stay in the waiting room during surgery. Go grab food nearby and they'll call when it's done.
  • Dr. Movahed will call after surgery to explain how it went
  • When you come to see me post-op, expect it to be unsettling. There will be blood and swelling. This is normal.
  • Bring a notebook or use the whiteboard to track fluids and protein intake from the start
