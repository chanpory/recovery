How you can help

Asking for help is hard for me, but I want to take this as an opportunity to lean on my community. This page is meant to make it easy. Even an hour or two makes a real difference. Jericho is 6 months old and Harold will be juggling his care with mine, so the most helpful thing anyone can do is give Harold a break.

While I’m in the hospital

April 9–11 (possibly April 12)

Harold will be at the hospital with me. His mom (in town April 6–13) and his sister (April 7–24) will be watching Jericho at home, but an extra pair of hands would be a big help so they can rest or step out.

  • Hold, play with, or watch Jericho for an hour or a few hours
  • Pick up groceries or supplies if anything runs low

First two weeks home

April 12–23

Harold’s mom (through April 13) and his sister (through April 24) will be helping, but Harold is still the primary person preparing all my meals, managing medications, and driving me to daily follow-up appointments in Walnut Creek. An extra person for even a couple of hours lets him focus on one thing at a time.

  • Spend time with Jericho while Harold preps meals or takes care of himself
  • Bring a meal for Harold (he will forget to feed himself)
  • Help with laundry, dishes, or tidying up

After April 24

Harold’s sister will have gone home

It’ll be just me, Harold, and Jericho. I’ll still have limited energy and mobility. This is when the support system shrinks and the fatigue catches up.

  • Time with Jericho, even for an hour or two
  • Grocery run or smoothie ingredient restock
  • Just being present so Harold isn’t doing everything alone

Bring a meal

Harold is eating normal food. I’m on liquids only for the first two weeks, then soft foods. I’m not allowed to chew for at least 2 months. If you’d like to bring something I can eat, anything that doesn’t require chewing works:

  • Soups and stews (blended or with very soft pieces)
  • Mashed potatoes, sweet potatoes, or refried beans
  • Scrambled eggs, soft tofu, or oatmeal
  • Well-cooked pasta with soft sauce
  • Smoothies, pudding, or yogurt
  • Anything you can easily pass a fork through

Anytime

These are always welcome:

  • Costco or grocery run (smoothie ingredients, formula, diapers)
  • A text to check in. I won’t be able to talk for a while, but I can read messages.

Just pick something from this page and text Harold to coordinate a time. Thank you.
