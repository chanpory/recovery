How you can help

Asking for help is hard for me, but I want to take this as an opportunity to lean on my community. This page is meant to make it easy. Even an hour or two makes a real difference. Jericho is 6 months old and Harold will be juggling his care with mine, so the most helpful thing anyone can do is give Harold a break.

First two days in the hospital

Thu Apr 9 – Sat Apr 11 (possibly Sun Apr 12)

Harold will be splitting time between the hospital and home with Jericho. His mom (in town Sun Apr 6–Sun Apr 13) and his sister (Mon Apr 7–Fri Apr 24) will be helping with Jericho, but an extra pair of hands would be a big help so they can rest or step out.

  • Hold, play with, or watch Jericho for an hour or a few hours
  • Pick up groceries or supplies if anything runs low

Weekend of Sat Apr 12 – Sun Apr 13

This is when we particularly need help. Harold’s mom leaves Sun Apr 13, and Harold will be juggling childcare while learning my medication schedule, meal prep routine, and post-surgery aftercare.

  • Spend time with Jericho so Harold can focus on getting me settled at home
  • Help with laundry, dishes, or tidying up
  • Bring a meal for Harold

First two weeks home

Mon Apr 14 – Thu Apr 23

Harold’s sister (through Fri Apr 24) will be helping, but Harold is still the primary person preparing all my meals, managing medications, and driving me to daily follow-up appointments in Walnut Creek. An extra person for even a couple of hours lets him focus on one thing at a time.

  • Spend time with Jericho while Harold preps meals or takes care of himself
  • Help with laundry, dishes, or tidying up
  • Drive me to a follow-up appointment in Walnut Creek. This frees Harold to stay home with Jericho in the mornings. See the appointments page for dates and times.

Weekend of Sat Apr 25 – Sun Apr 26

My niece is helping on weekdays, but this weekend it’ll be just me, Harold, and Jericho. I’ll still have limited energy and mobility.

  • Time with Jericho, even for an hour or two
  • Grocery run or smoothie ingredient restock
  • Just being present so Harold isn’t doing everything alone

Bring a meal for Harold

Harold is eating normal food and will forget to feed himself. Any meal he can heat up or eat right away is great. He likes veggie-forward, healthy meals and has no food allergies. I’m allergic to shellfish.

Bring a soft meal for me

After Sat Apr 26

I’m on liquids only for the first two weeks. After that I transition to soft foods, but I’m not allowed to chew for at least 2 months. Anything that doesn’t require chewing works:

  • Soups and stews (blended or with very soft pieces)
  • Mashed potatoes or sweet potatoes
  • Scrambled eggs, soft tofu, or oatmeal
  • Well-cooked pasta with soft sauce
  • Smoothies, pudding, or yogurt
  • Anything you can easily pass a fork through

Anytime

These are always welcome:

  • Come hang out. I won’t be able to talk much and someone may need to wipe the drool off my face, but company is nice and Harold could really use adult conversation.
  • A text or photo to check in. I can read messages even when I can’t respond.
  • Pick up a prescription or drop off supplies.

Just pick something from this page and text Harold to coordinate a time. Thank you.
