How you can help

I’m not great at asking for help, so Harold and I put this page together. Even a few hours makes a real difference. Jericho is 6 months old and Harold will be juggling his care with mine, so the most helpful thing anyone can do is give Harold a break.

While I’m in the hospital

April 9–11 (possibly April 12)

Harold will be at the hospital with me. His mom (in town April 6–13) and his sister (April 7–24) will be watching Jericho at home, but an extra pair of hands during the day would still be a huge help — even just 3–4 hours so they can rest or step out.

  • Hold, play with, or watch Jericho for a 3–4 hour block
  • Walk him in the stroller so the house is quiet
  • Pick up groceries or supplies if anything runs low

First two weeks home

April 12–23

Harold’s mom (through April 13) and his sister (through April 24) will be helping, but Harold is still the primary person preparing all my meals, managing medications, and driving me to daily follow-up appointments in Walnut Creek. An extra person for a few hours lets him focus on one thing at a time instead of everything at once.

  • Spend time with Jericho for 3–4 hours while Harold preps meals or takes care of himself — showering, eating, or getting outside for a bit
  • Bring a meal for Harold (he will forget to feed himself)
  • Help with laundry, dishes, or tidying up — a newborn and a liquid diet generate a surprising amount of both

Weekend of April 25–26

Harold’s sister will have gone home

It’ll be just me, Harold, and Jericho. I’ll still have limited energy and mobility. This is when the support system shrinks and the fatigue catches up.

  • Same as above — 3–4 hour blocks with Jericho are the most helpful thing
  • Grocery run or smoothie ingredient restock
  • Just being present so Harold isn’t doing everything alone

Anytime

These are always welcome and don’t need to be scheduled:

  • Drop off a meal for Harold (he’s eating normal food, I’m on liquids for the first two weeks, then soft foods I can eat without chewing)
  • Costco or grocery run — smoothie ingredients, formula, diapers
  • A text to check in (I won’t be able to talk for a while, but I can read messages)

Bring a meal I can eat

After April 23 · soft food phase

After the first two weeks, I’ll transition to soft foods — but I’m not allowed to chew for at least 2 months. If you’d like to bring a meal, anything I can swallow without chewing works:

  • Soups and stews (blended or with very soft pieces)
  • Mashed potatoes, sweet potatoes, or refried beans
  • Scrambled eggs, soft tofu, or oatmeal
  • Well-cooked pasta with soft sauce
  • Smoothies, pudding, or yogurt
  • Anything you can easily pass a fork through

You don’t need to ask what we need — just pick something from this list and text Harold to coordinate a time. Specific offers are easier for us to say yes to than “let me know if you need anything.” Thank you.
